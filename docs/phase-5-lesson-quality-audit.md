## Evidence and corrections

- Every lesson now begins with a different-example pre-check and ends with a post-transfer task.
- Predictions are committed before execution evidence is revealed; Conditions uses named branch choices.
- Recognition examples differ from the guided examples and keep structural evidence concealed until submission.
- Build scaffolds are valid Python but fail their semantic role verification unchanged.
- Build feedback is criterion-specific, and scenarios execute fresh copies of the learner's submitted source.
- Conditions and Loops most strongly require Lens because path and repeated-state evidence are otherwise difficult to inspect. Values and Functions remain useful without Lens, so their Lens-necessity score is intentionally conservative.

These scores establish content readiness for a small pilot. They are not evidence that learners improved.
